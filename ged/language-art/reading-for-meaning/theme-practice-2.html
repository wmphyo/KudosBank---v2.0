Theme Exercise 2

Question 1

In J.K. Rowling's 'Harry Potter' series, the theme of the power of love is a central element. Harry's ability to love deeply and his mother's sacrificial love protect him from the dark forces of Voldemort. The series illustrates how love can be a powerful force that conquers evil and brings people together.

Question 2

In George Orwell's 'Animal Farm', the theme of power and corruption is explored through the rise and fall of a group of farm animals who overthrow their human farmer. The pigs, who assume leadership, become increasingly corrupt and indistinguishable from the humans they replaced, highlighting how power can corrupt those who hold it.

Question 3

In J.D. Salinger's 'The Catcher in the Rye', the theme of teenage alienation and the loss of innocence is prevalent. The protagonist, Holden Caulfield, feels disconnected from the world around him and is critical of the phoniness he perceives in others. His desire to protect the innocence of children reveals his deep-seated fear of growing up and facing the harsh realities of adulthood.

Question 4

In Harper Lee's 'Go Set a Watchman', the theme of personal growth and confronting one's past is prominent. The protagonist, Scout Finch, returns to her hometown as an adult and grapples with the changing views of her father and her own evolving beliefs. The novel explores the complexities of identity and the process of self-discovery.

Question 5

In 'The Road' by Cormac McCarthy, the theme of survival in a post-apocalyptic world is central. The story follows a father and son as they navigate a desolate landscape, struggling to stay alive. Their journey highlights the bond between them and the hope that persists even in the bleakest circumstances.

2. Which of the following statements best represents the theme of 'Animal Farm'?

3. Which of the following statements best represents the theme of 'The Catcher in the Rye'?

4. Which of the following statements best represents the theme of 'Go Set a Watchman'?

5. Which of the following statements best represents the theme of 'The Road'?
