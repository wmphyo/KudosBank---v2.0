Summary Exercise 5

Question 1

In the city of Greenfield, there is a significant debate about the future of public transportation. The Greenfield Transit Authority (GTA) has proposed an expansion of the existing light rail system, aiming to reduce traffic congestion and pollution. However, some residents argue that the funds would be better spent on improving the city's bus network, which they believe would serve a larger portion of the population more effectively. The light rail supporters point to the long-term benefits of a more sustainable and modern transit system, while the bus network advocates emphasize the immediate need for better service and coverage. As the city council prepares to vote on the proposal, community forums have been filled with passionate discussions from both sides.

Question 2

Renewable energy sources, such as wind and solar power, are increasingly being adopted worldwide as nations seek to reduce their carbon footprints. These energy sources are praised for their ability to generate electricity without producing greenhouse gases. However, there are challenges associated with their implementation. Wind turbines and solar panels require significant initial investments, and their efficiency can be affected by geographic and weather conditions. Additionally, integrating renewable energy into existing power grids can be complex, requiring upgrades to infrastructure and changes in energy storage systems. Despite these challenges, the push for renewable energy continues to gain momentum as technological advancements make these sources more viable and cost-effective.

Question 3

The history of the internet is a fascinating tale of innovation and collaboration. Starting as a small network designed to share information among researchers, it has grown into a global platform that connects billions of people. The development of the World Wide Web in the early 1990s revolutionized the way we access and share information, leading to the rise of social media, e-commerce, and digital communication. As the internet continues to evolve, issues such as data privacy, cybersecurity, and the digital divide remain significant challenges. Despite these issues, the internet's impact on society is profound, influencing everything from education to entertainment to business.

Question 4

Climate change is one of the most pressing issues facing the world today. Scientists warn that if global temperatures continue to rise at the current rate, we will see more extreme weather events, rising sea levels, and disruptions to ecosystems. To combat climate change, many countries have pledged to reduce their greenhouse gas emissions by transitioning to renewable energy sources, increasing energy efficiency, and protecting forests that absorb carbon dioxide. However, achieving these goals requires international cooperation, substantial investment, and significant changes in policy and behavior. The urgency of the climate crisis has led to widespread activism and a growing recognition of the need for immediate action.

Question 5

The rise of remote work has transformed the traditional workplace. With advances in technology, employees can now work from anywhere, leading to greater flexibility and work-life balance. Companies benefit from reduced overhead costs and access to a broader talent pool. However, remote work also presents challenges, such as maintaining team cohesion, managing productivity, and ensuring data security. Additionally, not all jobs are suitable for remote work, and the shift can exacerbate inequalities for those without reliable internet access or a conducive work environment at home. As organizations navigate these challenges, they are developing new strategies to support remote workers and integrate remote work into their long-term plans.

1. Which of the following statements best summarizes the debate about public transportation in Greenfield?

2. Which of the following statements best summarizes the challenges and benefits of renewable energy sources?

3. Which of the following statements best summarizes the history and impact of the internet?

4. Which of the following statements best summarizes the global response to climate change?

5. Which of the following statements best summarizes the impact of remote work on the traditional workplace?
